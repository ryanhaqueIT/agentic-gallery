Forward-deployed engineering fails for one reason more than any other: context. The customer's systems, their pain, their numbers, and their politics live scattered across calls, docs, and repos — and by the time it's assembled into a slide deck, it's stale and unverifiable.

This project is a different shape: a four-stage agentic pipeline where each stage reads the previous artifact, does its work — account research, deterministic repo scans, transcript mining, demo-building on the HappyRobot platform — and regenerates a single live dashboard. A human gate sits between every stage: nothing advances without review, and nothing renders without a citation. Suggested and validated never look the same.

The first engagement through the pipeline is AIDO × HappyRobot — wiring the Wedding OS voice agent ("Emma") into an existing AWS backend with zero production risk: adapters ship dark, every flag defaults OFF, and go-live is one reversible switch.

The design principles under the hood: deterministic structure first, model judgment second (repo scans and platform state carry the facts; the model narrates); no quote, no claim (every insight is anchored to a verbatim citation); and human gates at every stage boundary — the pipeline proposes, the engineer disposes.
