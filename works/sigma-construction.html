SiteOps Dockets

An AI-native docket intelligence layer for heavy civil head contractors — built with Jacky Liang. Turning docket review into transparent, evidence-backed, exception-driven cost decisions.

Client: Jacky Liang
Heavy Civil Construction
Agentic AI
Operations view

Jacky Liang works inside the heavy civil construction world at Sigma — a world where a single tier-1 project runs hundreds of equipment, labour, materials, and services dockets per day across dozens of cost codes. The financial integrity of the project depends on assigning each docket to the correct cost code within 24 hours. Today that assignment is a manual, text-based, phone-heavy reconciliation between what was booked on the docket and what actually happened on site.

SiteOps Dockets is the product we're building together: an AI reasoning layer that sits above existing digital docket pipes (Docketbook, Virtual Superintendent) and below existing ERPs (Vista, Jonas, CMiC). It ingests dockets, enriches them with telematics and site geometry, and uses a stack of six agents — Intake, Correlation, Reasoning, Cost-Split, Supervisor-Query, and Orchestrator — to classify, explain, and split docket costs with every decision traceable back to the evidence it used.

The wedge is clear. Competitors solved the paper problem a decade ago, and the ledger problem before that. What nobody has solved is the reasoning layer that turns a digital docket into a correctly allocated financial event, automatically. SiteOps shifts the site engineer from data processor to exception approver — running the 80% of the queue unattended and flagging the 20% that actually needs human judgement.
